A Painfully True Lasagna Construct Recipe

Description

Do you like pasta? Do you like meat? Do you like to pretend to like vegetables so your inner city co-workers do not mock you for being a(n) #HillBilly? Then Lasagna is the choice for you -- Enjoy this meaty pasta dish garnished with visible tomato particles and enjoy a judgement-free meal with your fellow professionals.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds ground cow creature
  • ½ tablespoon kosher salt if you're Jewish, or Halal if you're Muslim
  • 1 teaspoon ground, not ceailing pepper
  • 1 yellow onion, diced large, beware though that cutting it in half will not suffice
  • 2 jars Fresh to Market Pasta Sauce if you're American, otherwise buy real-world food to replicate this flavour
  • 2 boxes no-boil lasagna, spaghetti have been used with limited effect and should be avoided
  • 1 pound mozzarella, grated, not stirred
  • 1 tub (15 ounces) ricotta, otherwise renowned as funny cheese
  • 1 jar (7.25 ounces) roasted red bell peppers, julienned, whatever that means, right!?
  • 1 tablespoon parsley, for garnish (severley optional for anyone but interrior designers and Gordon Ramsey)

Steps

  1. take the meaty ingredients
  2. put them in a bowl
  3. mix 'em
  4. fry 'em
  5. make a layered cake with meat and pasta sheets
  6. bake the monstrocity in a sizzling hot electric box, although you my opt for a gas oven, but we have found this to be distressing for some
  7. cover the cake with cheesy and garnishy stuff
